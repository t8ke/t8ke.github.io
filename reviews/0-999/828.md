
[**Review #828 | Scotch Review #279 - Glenrothes 20yr SMWS 30.100 - An Abundance of Fruit Cake**]( https://t8ke.review/review-828-scotch-malt-whisky-society-30-100-the-glenrothes-20yr-an-abundance-of-fruit-cake/)

**The Schtick:** This is a 20 year old bottling of distillate produced at The Glenrothes and bottled by the Scotch Malt Whisky Society after spending 20 years in an ex-sherry 2nd fill butt. 

-----

**Distiller:** Glenrothes

**Bottler:** SMWS

**Selection:** N/A

**ABV:** 56.5% ABV

**Age:** 20 Years 

-----

**Nose:**  Syrup, honey, toffee. Incredibly rich sherry. Nutty, figs, dates, nougat. Caramel, vanilla cream, sweet cocoa. 

**Taste:** Thick, rich. Raisins, honey, toffee, sherry. Caramel, nougat, dates, figs. Sweet, buttery popcorn. Some heat. One of the richest mouth feels that I have experienced yet. Theres some heat here, though.

**Finish:** Long, the richness continues. A bit of heat. Super sweet, loaded with honey roasted peanuts, caramel, toffee, nougat. Nutty, sweet sherry. Rich figs, dates, fruits out the wazoo.

**Overall:** If it werent for the heat in the palate / finish this would be a 10/10. That said, the richness is incredible here. The fruits are intense and the caramel / nougat sherry influence is just bonkers. Well done on this barrel, SMWS, well done. 

Thanks for reading!
